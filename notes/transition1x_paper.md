# Transition1x — methods refresh (Schreiner et al., Sci Data 2022)

*Schreiner, Bhowmik, Vegge, Busk, Winther. "Transition1x — a dataset for building
generalizable reactive machine learning potentials." Scientific Data 9:779 (2022).
DOI 10.1038/s41597-022-01870-w. Paper PDF in repo root.*

## What it is
9.6M DFT single points (energies + forces) on configurations **on and around
reaction paths**, generated by running NEB with DFT on ~10k organic reactions and
saving the intermediate images. Built specifically so ML potentials see
transition-state regions, not just near-equilibrium structures.

## Level of theory
- **ωB97X / 6-31G(d)** — chosen for compatibility with ANI1x, *not* for accuracy.
- **ORCA 5.0.2**, driven by **ASE 3.22.1**. (We use ORCA 6.1 — same functional/basis.)

## Source reactions
- From **Grambow et al.** reactant–product pairs, themselves derived from the
  **GDB7** enumeration. Up to **7 heavy atoms**, elements **C, N, O (+H)**.
- Grambow generated reactants/products/TSs for **11,961** reactions with the
  **Growing String Method (GSM)** at **ωB97X-D3/def2-TZVP** in Q-Chem.
- Neutral closed/open-shell CHNO; **no charge/spin field** stored.
- Up to **6 bond changes** per reaction (bonds breaking/forming between any heavy-atom pairs).

## Generation workflow (their Fig. 1)
1. **Relax** reactant and product with DFT until **F < 0.01 eV/Å**.
2. **Build the initial path with IDPP** — and crucially, in **two segments**:
   reactant→TS and TS→product, where **TS is the GSM transition state from the
   original data**. So their initial band is *seeded through the GSM TS*.
3. **NEB** with DFT until **Fmax < 0.5 eV/Å** (plain NEB first — climbing image off).
4. **CI-NEB** until **Fmax < 0.05 eV/Å**.
5. **Discard the whole reaction** if CI-NEB hasn't converged within **500 iterations**.
6. Save intermediate path iterations when **sufficiently different** (cumulative
   Fmax since the last saved path > 0.1 eV/Å).

## NEB settings
- **10 images**; spring constant **k = 0.1 eV/Å²**.
- Optimizer: **ASE BFGS, α = 70, max step 0.03 Å** — for *everything* (endpoint
  relaxation, NEB, CI-NEB).
- The stored **"transition_state" is the highest-energy CI-NEB image** at
  convergence.

## Outcome / validation
- **10,073 of 11,961** reactions converged (the rest discarded). 89% converged
  within the first 200 iterations.
- NEB (this work, 6-31G(d)) vs GSM (def2-TZVP) barriers: **MAE 0.16 eV, RMSE 0.19 eV**;
  TS-geometry **RMSD 0.11 Å** average.
- NEB barriers tend **lower** than GSM; "GSM was caught in sub-optimal reaction
  pathways more often than NEB" (more outliers above the y=x line).

## HDF5 structure
`data` / `train` / `val` / `test` groups → `{formula}` groups → `{rxn}` groups
(n configs, m atoms) → per-reaction `reactant` / `transition_state` / `product`
subgroups (n=1). Datasets: `atomic_numbers (m,)`, `wB97x_6-31G(d).energy (n,)`,
`...forces (n,m,3)`, `positions (n,m,3)`, `hash`.

## Implications for our flaw hunt

Three of their choices define exactly where our pipeline can do *more* than they did:

1. **They never refined the TS to a stationary point.** Stated explicitly: "No
   further refinement of the transition states was done… the goal was to generate
   a dataset of molecular configurations close to reaction pathways rather than
   finding accurate transition states." Their stored TS is just the highest
   CI-NEB image (perpendicular Fmax < 0.05 eV/Å on the band). So our **OptTS →
   Freq → IRC** chain genuinely goes beyond their procedure — there is real room
   for a lower/better saddle, and a Hessian on their TS legitimately can show
   extra small modes.

2. **Their initial band is seeded *through the GSM TS*; ours is not.** They IDPP
   the path in two segments anchored on the GSM transition state. Our baseline
   `nebskill-neb` IDPP-interpolates **reactant→product directly, with no TS seed**.
   So our initial guess is *less informed* than theirs — which is a strong
   candidate explanation for our **HIGHER deviators** (r11, r13): a worse starting
   path can settle the band on a worse (higher) saddle than their TS-seeded run
   found. **Actionable:** to reproduce their path faithfully (and likely fix the
   HIGHER cases), seed our NEB through the dataset's stored TS — e.g.
   `--ts-guess <their TS>` or an `--initial-path` that passes through it. This is
   the closest analog of their two-segment IDPP.

3. **No conformer search, ever.** Neither GSM nor their NEB explores TS
   conformers. So the GOAT-TS conformer idea targets a step their pipeline
   *structurally skipped* — the most systematically untouched source of
   over-estimated barriers, especially for larger/floppier molecules.

Also worth remembering: **6-31G(d) is a deliberately small basis** (ANI1x
compatibility), so absolute barriers are basis-limited and shifted high vs
def2-TZVP. We match their basis, so we *reproduce* them — but any "true" barrier
claim is at their (modest) level of theory, not a converged one.
